Identifying Abuse

Types of abuse

Recognizing the different forms of abuse is the first step in protecting yourself and others. Educate yourself on the signs and understand if you or someone you know may be affected (Hover over the card for more details).

Sexual Violence

Any unwanted sexual behavior, including stalking, grooming, indecent exposure, and non-consensual sexual activities.

Psychological Abuse

Threats, humiliation, intimidation, controlling behavior, and provoking fear.

Financial Abuse

Theft, fraud, coercion, or misuse of a person's finances, such as withholding benefits or pressuring for inheritance.

Institutional Abuse

Mistreatment or neglect in an organization, including poor care standards and violation of human rights.

Neglect

Failure to provide essential care, support, or necessities such as food, medication, and health services.

Exploitation

Taking advantage of a person for personal gain, including forced labor, servitude, and human trafficking.

Domestic Violence

Controlling, coercive, or violent behavior by an intimate partner or family member.

Human Trafficking

Forcibly moving and exploiting individuals for forced labor, sexual exploitation, or organ harvesting.

Hate Crime

Criminal acts motivated by prejudice based on race, religion, gender identity, or other factors.
